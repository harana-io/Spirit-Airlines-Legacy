The Flight Deck

Cockpit Panels

The Airbus A320 glass cockpit is split-crew operation with full fly-by-wire. For maintenance, knowing which system lives on which panel is essential.

🎛️
Overhead Panel (OHP)
Houses most aircraft systems: electrical buses, fuel pumps, hydraulic pump selectors, lighting, APU controls, fire protection handles, bleed air, pressurization, and ice protection switches. The "office ceiling" of maintenance.
🖥️
Full Instrument Panel
Six EFIS screens: two PFDs, two NDs, one upper ECAM (E/WD), one lower ECAM (SD). The SD rotates through system pages — hydraulics, fuel, elec, bleed, pressurization, doors, and more. 24 system pages total.
🖱️
Pedestal Panel
Engine thrust levers, TOGA, FLEX/MCT detents, reversers, speed brake lever, flap/slat lever, park brake, ATC transponder, ACARS/SATCOM controls, and the MCDU (Multifunction Control Display Unit) for FMS entries.
◀️
Left Instrument Panel
Captain's side: PFD, ND, ISIS (standby), compass, weather radar, GPWS controls, sidestick. Also contains the left audio control panel for VHF 1, HF, PA, and interphone comms (ATA 23).
▶️
Right Instrument Panel
First Officer's side: mirror of captain's PFD and ND. Contains the KCCU (Keyboard Cursor Control Unit) and MCDU. Right audio control panel for VHF 2, VHF 3, and navigation radio tuning.
⚠️
ECAM / Caution System
The E/WD upper display shows engine parameters and alerts. Lower SD shows the selected system page. ECAM actions (checklists) appear automatically. A&P techs use the CFDS (Centralized Fault Display System) via the MCDU for fault isolation.

⚡ ATA 24 Note

Panels 2000VU and 2001VU on the overhead are high-voltage areas. Exercise caution when opening — always verify power state via ECAM electrical page before working in these zones.

Digital Tools

The Tech Toolkit

Modern A320 maintenance goes beyond the hangar — Airbus's digital ecosystem gave you access to the full technical library, fault isolation, and maintenance documentation in real time.

🌐
Airbus World
Central portal for all Airbus technical documentation. Access via w3.airbus.com through Citrix. Gateway to AirnavX, Airman-web, service bulletins, and the full AMM library.
🔍
AirnavX
Airbus navigational troubleshooting tool for fault isolation on A320 family aircraft. Use it to trace ECAM warnings back to root-cause LRUs. Spirit used v1.4 with the new troubleshooting function module.
📚
Airman-web
The Airbus Maintenance Manual access interface. Real-time AMM, IPC, TSM, and AWM publication access. Includes auto-learning tool for procedure training. Direct pipeline to every maintenance task card.
🔥
MX Hot Topics
Spirit's internal Tech Ops knowledge share page — accessed via Spirit Link → Operations → Technical Operations → Maintenance Training. A living library of ramp wisdom and in-service lessons learned.
📊
Load 15 Report
Spirit's internal maintenance trend and reliability report. Learning to read a Load 15 meant understanding how the fleet was performing systemically — MELs, deferred defects, and repeat write-ups at a fleet level.
✈️
Flight Phases
The A320 defines 10 flight phases from engine start to final shutdown. Critical for maintenance — knowing which phase a defect occurred in narrows fault isolation dramatically. Phase-specific vibrations, transients, and faults each tell a story.

Spirit Airlines History

1983 — 2024

From a charter airline in Michigan to a 200-aircraft ultra-low-cost giant — and finally, one last gate close. Here's how it unfolded.

1983
Founded as Charter One Airlines
Incorporated in Eastpointe, Michigan. Initial operations as a charter carrier with a small fleet of piston-engine aircraft.
1990
Rebranded to Spirit Airlines
The Spirit name took hold. The airline began its expansion toward scheduled service and started building the identity that would become iconic in American aviation.
1994
All-Jet Airline Transition
Spirit completed its transformation to an all-jet carrier, operating DC-9s and MD-80s. The yellow brand identity began to solidify around this era.
2001
Headquarters: Miramar, Florida
Spirit relocated its headquarters to Miramar, FL — solidifying its identity as a Florida-based carrier focused on sun and value destinations throughout the Americas.
2007
First Airbus Orders — A319 & A320
Spirit signed its first major Airbus order, beginning the transition that would eventually result in a 100% A320 family fleet. The yellow Airbus era had begun.
2011
IPO & 100% Airbus Fleet
Spirit went public on the NYSE (SAVE) and completed the transition to an all-Airbus A320 family fleet — the standardization that made maintenance training like the 40-hour Gen Fam possible.
2014
A321 Enters Spirit Service
The larger A321ceo joined the fleet, opening higher-density routes. Spirit became one of the densest-configured operators in the world — maximizing every square foot of cabin.
2017
A320neo Family Arrives
The A320neo with PW1100G-JM Geared Turbofan engines entered the Spirit fleet — introducing a whole new propulsion architecture that A&P techs had to master alongside the classic V2500.
2021
40-Hour Gen Fam Course #108T21
The A320 General Familiarization course that this archive documents. Built by Spirit Tech Ops Training, revised by David Jones. ATA chapters 21 through 71 — the complete aircraft in five days.
2022–23
Acquisition Bids: Frontier & JetBlue
Frontier Airlines made a merger bid, then JetBlue made a higher offer. The JetBlue-Spirit merger was ultimately blocked by the U.S. Department of Justice in January 2024 on antitrust grounds.
NOV 2024
Final Flight — Spirit Ceases Operations
Spirit Airlines filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection and subsequently ceased all operations. The yellow fleet was grounded. After 41 years, the last Spirit gate closed. But the Spirit — and the people who kept those jets flying — lives on.
